Brief Summary of the Research Area

Traditionally, 3D modelling of the face has consisted of models learning an unfufilled representation of a face, using linear transformations or using vast numbers of matrices to hold vast amounts of data.

The years of research has led to exciting and engaging animations in both movies and video games and facial recognition software has also advanced. Faces can be very easily represented using linear models. However, due to the linearity of these models, they cannot accurately represent non-linear deformations. The human face is a very complex shape and it is affected by various many factors; and most significantly it deforms a great deal with any change in expression.

Changes in expression (deformations in 3D model) are very important when capturing the realism of a 3D face. This is extremely difficult and unfeasible to do using the old, traditional methods. Hence, a different approach is required - Convolution Neural Networks (CNNs). Modelling convolutions on 3D meshes is memory efficient, resulting in high resolution 3D structures to be processed. A potential issue is the lack of 3D datsets available for training the neural network, but we discuss how to overcome this specific problem to a some certain extent on a different webpage.

Through the use of Big Data, a program can also look at a 2D image and classify different body parts, and construct the appropriate 3D image. This method also reduces the huge storage needed for the matrices representing the 3D body.

While the traditional approach has been very effective in the past years, there is substantial research that has to be carried out in order to implement 3D body modelling as efficiently as possible, and AI perhaps holds the key to unlocking a much more optimal approach.
